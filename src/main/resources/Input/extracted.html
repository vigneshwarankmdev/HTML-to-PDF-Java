IMPORTANT SAFETY INFORMATION
\n\t\t\t\t
CONTRAINDICATIONS
\n \t\t\t\t

Known hypersensitivity to benralizumab or excipients.

\n \t\t\t\t
WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS
\n \t\t\t\t

Hypersensitivity Reactions
\nHypersensitivity reactions (eg, anaphylaxis, angioedema, urticaria, rash) have occurred after administration of FASENRA. These reactions generally occur within hours of administration, but in some instances have a delayed onset (ie, days). Discontinue in the event of a hypersensitivity reaction.

\n

Acute Asthma Symptoms or Deteriorating\nDisease
FASENRA should not be used to treat acute asthma symptoms, acute exacerbations, or acute bronchospasm.

\n

Reduction of Corticosteroid Dosage
\nDo not discontinue systemic or inhaled corticosteroids abruptly upon initiation of therapy with FASENRA. Reductions in corticosteroid dose, if appropriate, should be gradual and performed under the direct supervision of a physician.\nReduction in corticosteroid dose may be associated with systemic withdrawal symptoms and/or unmask

\n \t\t\t
\n \t\t\t
\n \t\t\t\t

conditions previously suppressed by systemic corticosteroid therapy.

\n \t\t\t\t

Parasitic (Helminth) Infection
\nIt is unknown if FASENRA will influence a patient's response against helminth infections. Treat patients with pre-existing helminth infections before initiating therapy with FASENRA. If patients become infected while receiving FASENRA and do not respond to anti-helminth treatment, discontinue\nFASENRA until infection resolves.

\n
ADVERSE REACTIONS
\n

The most common adverse reactions (incidence\n≥ 5%) include headache and pharyngitis.

\n

Injection site reactions (eg, pain, erythema, pruritus, papule) occurred at a rate of 2.2% in patients treated with FASENRA compared with 1.9% in patients treated with placebo.

\n
USE IN SPECIFIC POPULATIONS
\n

A pregnancy exposure registry monitors pregnancy outcomes in women exposed to FASENRA during pregnancy. To enroll call 1-877-311-8972 or visit\nwww.mothertobaby.org/fasenra.

\n

The data on pregnancy exposure from the clinical trials are insufficient to inform on drug-associated risk. Monoclonal antibodies such as benralizumab

\n \t\t\t
\n
\n\t

are transported across the placenta during the third trimester of pregnancy; therefore, potential effects on a fetus are likely to be greater during the third trimester of pregnancy.

FASENRA is indicated for the add-on maintenance treatment of patients with severe asthma aged 6 years and older, and with an eosinophilic phenotype.
